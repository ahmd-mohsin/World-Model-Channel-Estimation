# ContextEncoder feature analysis

Generated by `visualize_encoder.py` (run in the `wireless` venv). Figures in `figures/`.
The goal: understand **what the frozen LWM-backed encoder actually encodes** about a channel,
before any pipeline training. All channels are synthetic MIMO-OFDM with controlled physics
(number of paths, angle-of-departure, delay spread, SNR).

## Figures

### `01_input_channels.png` — the inputs
Real/imag of the channel grid (antenna × subcarrier) for 4 regimes. Clear visual structure:
single-path = clean steering stripes; more paths = richer interference; low SNR = visible noise
floor. This is what `o_t` looks like before encoding.

### `03_parameter_sweep.png` — the strongest evidence (controlled, one variable at a time)
Hold path gain and delay **fixed**, sweep only the angle-of-departure: the frozen LWM feature
vector traces a **smooth, continuous trajectory**. Same for sweeping only the delay. This shows
the encoder is not hashing inputs randomly — it maps physically-nearby channels to nearby
embeddings, and the periodic shape matches array-steering / delay-phase geometry. **This is the
property the downstream SSM needs: a smooth latent the dynamics model can interpolate over.**

### `02_pca_lwm_features.png` — global structure (2-D PCA of the embeddings)
- **PC1 (72% variance) cleanly tracks SNR** — the dominant axis of variation the encoder
  captures is channel quality / noise level.
- Number-of-paths and AoD are **not** separated in the top 2 PCs — they live in higher
  dimensions and are entangled. Honest read: the raw frozen features surface SNR most strongly.

### `04_linear_probe.png` — quantitative read-out (5-fold CV linear probe)
| Property | Score | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| SNR (30 vs 10 dB) | **0.69** acc | recoverable above chance |
| rich-vs-sparse (#paths≥8) | 0.61 acc | weakly recoverable |
| #paths (log2 regression) | **0.00** R² | **not** linearly recoverable |

## Honest conclusions

1. **The encoder works and is well-behaved**: deterministic, smooth in physical parameters,
   and shape-correct (`o_t (B,T,2,32,32) → x_t (B,T,256)`).
2. **Out of the box, the frozen LWM features most strongly encode SNR / channel quality**, with
   path richness only weakly linearly accessible and exact path-count not linearly decodable.
3. **Why path-count is weak:** (a) LWM was pretrained on DeepMIMO ray-traced channels with its
   own normalization; our synthetic channels are out-of-distribution. (b) We read raw frozen
   features with a *linear* probe and an *untrained* projection head — the head is random until
   the JEPA pipeline trains it. We should not expect strong task structure yet.
4. **What this means for the pipeline:** the encoder is a sound, smooth feature source. Whether
   it is *good enough* for channel estimation is a question to answer empirically once the
   projection head is trained end-to-end (and/or by fine-tuning LWM, or feeding real DeepMIMO
   data instead of synthetic). The probe here is a baseline to beat, not a final verdict.
